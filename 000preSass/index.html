A Little About Me

Holly Silvia is a graphic designer and teacher with a passion for illustration and design. She has a B.S. in Computer Science with an Emphasis in Digital Art and a M.F.A. in Media Design. When she’s freelancing, she mostly works on custom art prints, stationary, and branding projects. At Butler she teaches classes related to digital art, web development, and skills students need to succeed in the creative design field. In between jobs she works on a learning video series called Teach Me That and experiments with various heat transfer crafts.

Module 4: Adding Accessibility

I have gone through my entire site and made changes so it’s accessible. The goal is to make the site friendly for users with vision, auditory, mobility, or cognitive impairments. Images have alt text, colors have good contrast, aria has been added where necessary, and various other structure changes have been made so the pages work well with accessibility tools. If you hit tab on any page and go to the top, you will find a “Skip to Content link” which bypasses the navigation(s) and goes right to the main content.

Helpful Resources:

  • Aditus Multiple Navigation Landmarks
  • Deque University: Screen Reader Keyboard Shortcuts and Gestures
